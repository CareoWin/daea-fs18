Karnak - Precinct of Mut

18th Dynasty - Ptolemaic Period

Emma Wilhelm

Introduction

The Precinct of Mut is a temple complex in the larger Karnak temple complex in modern day Luxor (ancient Thebes). It was dedicated to the Goddess Mut, who was a protector goddess, wife to the God Amun, and mother of the moon god Khonsu. She often appeared in the form of Sekhmet, a female deity with the head of a lion, whom a great many statues have been found at the cite. The earliest datable remains at the cite date to shared reign of Hatshepsut and her nephew/step-son, Thutmose III during the 18th dynasty. The site was added to by many successive Pharaohs, and into the Ptolemaic Period.

In addition the main Mut Temple, the complex also houses a another large tempel, called Temple A, and large chapel, a temple built by Ramasses III, a gateway named by its builder, King Taharqa, and other small temples and statues.



Location

The Precinct of Mut is located in what was the ancient city of Thebes, which is mostly cobered by the modern city of Luxor.

The Precinct of Mut is located a hundred yards south of the Temple of Amun, which the complex faces. In addition, the two complexes are connected by a long avenue of Sphinxs. Around the Mut Temple itself is a large lake called Isheru, which is sacred to a group of goddesses to which Mut belongs, called the Eye of Re.
